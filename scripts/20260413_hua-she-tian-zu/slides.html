huà    shé    tiān    zú
画蛇添足
成语意思：
huà
画
① 画画、绘画  to draw / paint
② 用笔描绘图形  to depict with a brush or pen
shé
蛇
蛇，没有脚的爬行动物  a snake — a reptile with no legs
tiān
添
① 增加、加上  to add / increase
② 本身中性，在此为多余之举  neutral in itself, but here an unnecessary extra
zú
足
① 脚、腿  foot / leg
② 足够  sufficient / enough
成语意思：
字面意思
画完了蛇，还要多此一举地给蛇加上脚。
After finishing a drawing of a snake, unnecessarily adding feet to it.
引申义
事情已经做好了，却多此一举地加上没有必要的东西，
反而坏了事。
Something is already done well, but you add unnecessary extras that end up ruining it.
成语由来：
《战国策·齐策二》
作者：西汉 · 刘向  ·  著名学者、文献学家
记录战国时期各国谋臣策士的言行与政治活动的史书
A historical work recording the words and political activities
of advisors and strategists during the Warring States period
成语故事：
楚国有人祭祀完祖先，把一壶酒赏给了几个家仆。
家仆们商量：这酒几个人喝不够，一个人喝刚刚好。
不如比赛画蛇huà shé，谁先画完，谁就喝这壶酒。
In Chu, someone finished ancestral offerings and gave a jug of wine to several servants.
They discussed: not enough for all, but just right for one person.
Let's race to draw a snake — whoever finishes first gets to drink the wine.
成语故事：
一人很快画完，正要喝酒，却说：「我还能给蛇添足tiān zú！」
就在他画脚时，另一人也画完，夺过酒壶说：
「蛇本来就没有脚，你画的脚算什么？」——把酒喝了个精光。
One person quickly finished and was about to drink, but said: "I can even add feet!"
While he drew feet, another finished and snatched the jug:
"Snakes have no feet — what do your feet count for?" And drank every last drop.
近义词 & 反义词：
近义词
多此一举
duō cǐ yī jǔ
doing something completely unnecessary
弄巧成拙
nòng qiǎo chéng zhuō
trying to be clever but making things worse
反义词
恰到好处
qià dào hǎo chù
just right — not too much, not too little
锦上添花
jǐn shàng tiān huā
a welcome addition that makes something even better
例句：
这篇文章结尾已经很有力了，
你再加那段话，简直是画蛇添足huà shé tiān zú。
The ending of this essay was already powerful —
adding that extra paragraph is simply 画蛇添足.
好的表达就是「刚刚好」。多说一句，反而削弱了原本的力量。
例句：
面试表现得很好，最后却忍不住多说了几句，
结果画蛇添足huà shé tiān zú，给面试官留下了不好的印象。
The interview was going really well, but he couldn't help adding a few more sentences —
画蛇添足, and it left the interviewer with a bad impression instead.
记住：画蛇添足不是不够努力，而是努力过了头。

使用注意：
✓
贬义词，用来批评做了多余的事情，反而坏了事
A negative expression — criticizing an unnecessary act that ruins what was already good
✓
重点不只是「多余」，而是「因为多余而弄坏了原本好的结果」
如果多做一步没有影响，就不一定要用这个成语
Key: not just 'unnecessary' — but 'unnecessary AND it ruins what was already good'.
If the extra step causes no harm, this idiom may not fit.
✓
常用来提醒别人或自己：适可而止，不要过度
Often used to remind others or oneself: know when to stop — don't overdo it
